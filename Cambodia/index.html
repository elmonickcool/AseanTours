What to do in Cambodia

Thomb Raider Temple

Ta Prohm is the modern name of the temple in Siem Reap, Cambodia, built in the Bayon style largely in the late 12th and early 13th centuries and originally called Rajavihara. It is famous for its tree roots intertwining with the temple's stone structures, creating a unique and atmospheric setting.

Angkor Wat

Angkor Wat is a magnificent temple complex in Siem Reap, Cambodia. It is the largest religious monument in the world and a UNESCO World Heritage site. Built in the 12th century, it is renowned for its stunning architecture, intricate carvings, and cultural significance as a symbol of Cambodia.

Phnom Penh

Phnom Penh, the capital city of Cambodia, offers a blend of rich history and vibrant modernity. Explore the Royal Palace, visit the sobering Tuol Sleng Genocide Museum, or take a relaxing stroll along the scenic riverfront. Phnom Penh is a captivating destination that showcases the country's past and present.

Angkor Night Market

The Angkor Night Market in Siem Reap is a vibrant hub for shopping, dining, and experiencing the local culture. Browse through a wide range of handicrafts, clothing, and souvenirs, and savor delicious Cambodian street food. The lively atmosphere and unique offerings make it a must-visit spot in the city.
